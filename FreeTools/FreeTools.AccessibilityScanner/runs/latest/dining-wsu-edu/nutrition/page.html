    Dietary Needs & Allergens

    Dining is committed to the well-being and safety of students when it comes to allergens. That is why we are dedicated to working with students, staff, and guests who have food allergies by providing safe options for them at each dining center. 

    With several platforms that offer customers the chance to build their own dishes, our Dining Centers can provide a variety of vegetarian, vegan, gluten-friendly, or allergen-friendly options. If you have questions regarding ingredients in a specific menu item while in the dining centers, please talk to the chef or manager on duty. If you are having an allergic reaction, seek medical help immediately. 

    Labeling

    Dining Services labels the top 9 Common Allergens. At each dining center you will find that every food served will have a menu card with the top allergens and icons to assist customers in finding safe foods. The icons are below.

    To limit icons on menu cards, we cross-use some icrons. If you need specifics on an item (like if it has shellfish vs. fish), feel free to check NetNutrition or ask a chef or manager on duty.

    Specialty Stations

    Allergen-Friendly Options

    Dining Services labels all of the top nine most common allergens. This messaging is on signage in all the Dining Centers, or on our online menu and NetNutrition. Our Natural station at each dining center is a designated allergen-friendly, gluten-friendly zone where all dishes are made without using the top nine allergens and prepared separately to minimize the risk of cross contact.

    Gluten-Friendly and Gluten-Free Options

    We are always happy to provide customers with gluten allergies, gluten intolerance, or celiac disease with options. The Natural Station at each dining center offers a variety of gluten-free meal options that are prepared separately from other dishes, minimizing the risk for cross contact with trace amounts of gluten. Each dining center also keeps individually wrapped gluten-free breads, bagels, muffins, and desserts, on hand that can be distributed to customers as needed. Our staff can also prepare burgers, sandwiches, and pizzas using gluten-free products on request.

    Other Dietary Needs

    Halal Options

    We continue to strive to increase our number of halal proteins. We label items that do not contain alcohol (from ingredients like vanilla extract, vinegar, and/or miso paste), pork, or a non-halal protein with a halal icon to make it easier for our customers to find halal options. All dining centers offer: 

    • Boneless chicken breasts and thighs used in soups, Naturals stations, entrees, and the salad bar
    • Halal pepperoni is available on pizzas
    • Meals prepared to ensure Halal handling and separation from non‑Halal foods

    Vegan and Vegetarian

    We are proud to offer a variety of plant-based options to meet the needs of flexitarians, vegetarian, vegans, or anyone else who just wants to eat a little less meat! Plant-based cuisine is much more than vegetables from the salad bar. With a little creativity and some help from tofu, beans and legumes, non-dairy milks, seeds, nuts, and products from Gardein and Daiya, we are proud to offer everything from healthy tofu scrambles to indulgent deep-fried cauliflower wings and vegan chocolate brownies. All of our bakery's cobblers and fruit crisps are vegan-friendly, and we also serve vegan cookies, cakes, and bars. In 2018, we were recognized with an “A” rating on peta2’s vegan report card for “going above and beyond to provide all students with exceptional vegan food.”

    Vegetarian

    Does not contain meat, fish, poultry, or gelatin. Dairy and eggs are allowed.

    Vegan

    Vegetarian items that also do not contain dairy, eggs, or honey.

    Halal

    Does not contain pork, alcohol, or meat that is not halal certified. Fish and vegetarian proteins are permitted. 

    Dietary Accommodations

    Some food allergies may require further assistance with eating on campus. You can take the following steps to request dietary accommodations:

    • Contact our Dining Services Registered Dietitian, Martha Flores, at 509-335-4785 or martha.flores@wsu.edu
    • Contact Student Accommodations and Disability Resources and schedule an appointment to discuss housing and dining needs.

    Our team have worked with students to successfully meet many dietary needs, from PKU to a range of allergies.

    Student Testimonial: Hannah's Story

    Hannah, a WSU student and member of the Student Leadership Advisory Board, shares how the work of our Dining Services team has impacted her experience:

    “Hello everyone, I’m Hannah. I am one of the students who benefits directly from the work that Martha [Assistant Director of Health & Wellness and the Dining Registered Dietitan] and her team do, and I also serve on the Student Leadership Advisory Board.

    As someone with significant dietary restrictions, navigating day-to-day life on campus can be overwhelming. Having reliable, safe, and consistent food options has changed my entire experience as a student. It has made a huge difference in the variety and accessibility of my meals, and in how supported and understood I feel as a student who manages challenges that most students never have to face.

    Because of the care that goes into this work, I can start each day knowing I will have the nutrition I need to focus on my classes, my labs, and everything else I’m involved in. That stability is something I never take for granted. I want to thank all of you, no matter what department you work in, because support for programs like this is only possible when many different teams are behind it. Whether it is planning, communication, maintenance, technology, or anything in between, it truly makes a difference in the lives of students like me.

    Thank you for everything you do to help make this campus a place where students with unique needs can feel supported, included, and able to succeed.”
