    Welcome, Cougs


    Student Affairs is where your WSU experience comes to life.

    We’re here to foster student learning, development, and well-being — in and out of the classroom, on and off campus. In partnership with the academic mission, we create the spaces where students live, eat, learn, lead, work, play, and grow. From connecting with friends and exploring interests to finding mentors, gaining employment experience, and accessing resources when you need them, our programs and services are designed to help you thrive in every part of your Cougar journey.

    Our mission is simple: For all students, every day.

    As part of our mission to serve all students, every day we have a three-year action plan to guide our work. Track our progress and reach out if you have questions, ideas or if you want to collaborate.  

    Start Here

    If you have questions or need assistance, we can connect you with the resources you need to make your Coug experience the best it can be.

    Avalanche Awareness Clinic

    Location: Outdoor Recreation Center
    Sponsor: University Recreation

    Program Dates & Times:  2/17/2026 6:00pm - 8:00pm Registration Deadline: 2/17/2026 6:00pm Program Description: Know before you go! Following the American Avalanche Association guidelines, this clinic is an introduction to avalanche safety. We’ll look at avalanche safety through the lens of terrain, weather, snowpack, and human factors. Learn how to find and interpret weather forecasts and avalanche advisory information and identify strategies for decision-making and risk management. We’ll also discuss equipment for winter travel. This is an excellent first step on the journey into the backcountry. If you are a skier or snowboarder who is excited about venturing out of the resort and into the backcountry or side-country, then this clinic is a must. A potentially life-threatening, emergency situation is no time to be unprepared.

    Friday, February 20, 2026 - Sunday, February 22, 2026 @ 7:00 AM

    Wallowa Mountains Avalanche Awareness & Backcountry Ski

    Location: Outdoor Recreation Center
    Sponsor: University Recreation

    Program Dates & Times:  2/20/2026 7:00am - 2/22/2026 9:00pm Registration Deadline: 2/15/2026 11:55pm Trip Description: This course is designed for advanced downhill skiers/snowboarders with backcountry skiing/splitboarding experience, eager to advance their avalanche safety and touring skills. We’ll stay in yurts at Wallowa Lake State Park and embark on 2-3 day-tours into the Wallowa Mountains, where you’ll learn or review how to skin up and ski down in a premier backcountry setting. Learn from qualified instructors about the equipment necessary for winter backcountry travel, how to interpret local mountain weather forecasts and avalanche advisory information, how to assess avalanche terrain, and ways to manage risks while maximizing the fun factor. Learn/refresh your avalanche rescue skills, using beacon, shovel, and probe.



    Mandatory Pre-Trip Meeting: The pre-trip meeting will occur on Tuesday February 17th, from 6:00pm-9:00pm in the Outdoor Recreation Center. It occurs simultaneously with the Avalanche Awareness Clinic that night. Duplicate registration not required.



    Trip Rating: Level 4. Good physical conditioning is required, and competency in downhill skiing/snowboarding on steep slopes and in trees is required. Prior experience with backcountry skiing/splitboarding and/or avalanche awareness + rescue course/skills strongly recommended (our Intro to Backcountry Ski and Avalanche Rescue Course day trips are excellent prep!). Please review the FAQ's and the minimum requirements to participate on our website to help decide if this trip is appropriate for you.

    Lead Clinic

    Location: Climbing Wall
    Sponsor: University Recreation



    Are you getting comfortable top-roping, and want to learn a new skill that you can apply outside? Lead climbing is the method of climbing where you clip your rope into protection as you ascend, rather than being belayed through one anchor at the top of the climb (top roping). In this clinic we’ll teach you the skills needed to effectively lead climb and belay.







    Program rating is level 1. Climbing experience recommended.







    Cancellation Policy: Your clinic may be cancelled if at least 3 participants are not registered for this clinic. You can anticipate notice of cancellation 48 hours in advance.

    Monday, March 2, 2026 @ 12:00 AM

    "FEEL-HOW?" ASL Emotions Workshop

    Location: Terrell Library B20
    Sponsor: Disabled Student Center

    Join the Student Accommodations and Disability Resource Center for an engaging workshop led by our American Sign Language (ASL) Interpreters and DSC Assistant Coordinator Trina!

    This introductory session will explore how to express emotions through ASL. We'll begin with a brief overview of respectful and equitable communication with Deaf individuals, followed by a lesson on the role of facial expressions in ASL grammar, especially when conveying emotions. After the introduction, the workshop will transition to a voices-off environment, where participants will practice expressing their feelings using only non-verbal communication. Questions and responses during this portion will be written down to maintain a fully non-vocal experience.

    Snacks and drinks will be provided! Come ready to learn, connect, and express yourself in new ways.

    Please contact dsc@wsu.edu for questions or event accommodations.

    Join us weekly at the DSC Study Café, a neurodivergent-friendly environment designed to support your unique study style.
